CAREER HISTORY

Vitaco Health Australia Pty Ltd
August 2010 - Present
Senior Graphic Designer / Production Manager

As the sole designer across 7 brands, Aussie Bodies, Healtheries, Balance Sports Nutrition, Wagner, Bodytrim, Abundant Earth and Musashi my primary task has been taking a design from briefing to design and then into production. The majority of my work has been in the FMCG space with brand such as Aussie Bodies, Healtheries, Bodytrim and Abundant Earth. Working closely with brand managers taking their ideals and making them into exciting usable functional products. On any day my work can vary from trade promotion design, packaging, art directing photoshoots, outdoor and online campaigns development including html5 and flash as well as social media engagement. Many of the brands are sold into different countries like China and Saudi Arabia so each market presents different design challenges which I have to overcome. Being a sole designer has many challenges including the ability to adapt, manage many projects at different stages of production and prioritising jobs. Good communication and presentation skills are also essential as well as the ability to learn and development my skills as new projects arise and different skills are needed to aid development and design.

As part of my production manager role I liaise with packaging and print companies from initial quoting and keyline development to proofing, printing and press approvals, with each product having a different print requirements. I am confident in working across many printing process including digital, offset, gravure and screen. I also work closely with external media and advertising agencies depending on the requirements for a individual campaign.

Also my role as Creative Team Leader included assisting the advertising manager with day to day resource allocation and overseeing the designers workload.

Best & Less
October 2003 - August 2006
Senior Graphic Designer

Working in-house in the marketing and advertising department as the senior designer my role covered all aspects of retail design, including such areas as brand development, catalogues, point of sale design, visual merchandising, magazine and press ad design, product development, promotional design as well as signage and store frontage design for the 160 Best & Less stores throughout Australia. One of my main responsibility was designing all catalogues as well as point of sale used to advertise products in over 160 Best & Less stores. During my time I produced over 45 catalogues. My role meant that I would take a catalogue from brief, to presentation of layout, photo shoot, design, production and printing of each catalogue. Throughout this whole process I would liaise with an art director from our creative agency to achieve the best results.

My role as the senior designer meant that I was actively involved in the planning and art directing over 45 photo shoots. During a photo shoot I would be involved in all aspects of the creative process, from concepts to setup up of shot lists, model castings to visual direction of shots. A photo shoot could last anywhere from 1 to 5 days involving many models in either studio or location. My role also included setup and design of product shots such as bedroom and bathroom displays or individual items. A creative flair was always needed as each shoot required a specific feel. Throughout this whole process I would work closely with the photographer ensuring the ideas and concepts of the marketing and advertising department were captured. Being on hand at the printers when the catalogue was at press, I consider myself to have excellent knowledge of pre press and print issues. I was also responsible for signing off and making any final colour corrections on a print run that would number in the millions.

My role also included maintaining our website, point of sale design, store opening catalogues, design and production of promotional material such as gift cards and design of competition material such as competition strips for catalogues and competition entry forms. All these projects meant that I needed excellent knowledge and had extensive use of such programs as InDesign, Photoshop, Illustrator, Quickcut as well as web based and multimedia programs such as Go Live, Dreamweaver, Flash and After Effects.
